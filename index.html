Profile.

Casandra McDonald is a NN/g Master Certified UX / UI Designer and Front-End Web Engineer, with over 10+ years of experience based in New York City. Her goal is to help business grow by designing and developing web and native applications. She currently runs her own consulting business as a designer and developer for clients based in NYC, specializing in the finance and e-commerce industries.

As a UX Designer, I believe in user-centric design, and enforce those principles even throughout the design process. I follow the double diamond framework when starting any UX project: Discover, Design, Develop, Deliver. I work with companies on business strategy, perform competitive analysis, conduct user interviews, contextual inquiries, and usability testing. I also develop user personas, user flows, and feature prioritization with MVP focus. I possess a keen understanding of market dynamics that impact the successful launch of applications.

As a UI Designer, I follow all up to date HCI and W3C guidelines for accessibly on desktop and mobile applications. I would describe my work as clean, beautiful, and asthetically pleasing. I conform to a "less is more" methodology and apply that approach to reduce cognitive friction throughout all of my design deliverables. I create sketches and low, mid, and high fidelity mockups using Adobe XD and am well-versed in creating extremely detailed interactive prototypes with custom animations using XD and Principle. I frequently work with development teams to ensure pixel perfect asset delivery and create follow up documentation and style guides.

As a Front-End Engineer, my goal is to write clean, semantic, accessible code. I work with HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, and jQuery and PHP. I have hand coded over 200+ responsive websites and have experience building apps and APIs with JS, Firebase, Node.js, Express and Heroku. I am currently in school at Columbia University Software Engineering Bootcamp until Summer 2021 to expand my existing skillset in the Full Stack Development realm.
